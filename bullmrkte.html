Summary

In our competitive job market students are underprepared and do not have the necessary skills or experience they will need to find a job. Finding a meaningful career with a livable income is important for everyone to have dignity and enjoy life.

sentiment_very_dissatisfied

Problem

High School students, college students, and adult learners of all ages want to find meaningful careers. The problem is students do not know the proper career roadmap or skills needed to attain many of the jobs that are in demand. Students need guidance and mentorship so they can navigate the complex job market.

sentiment_very_satisfied

Solution

Bullmrkte is a platform for students to view career roadmaps and seek mentorship and guidance for careers they are interested in pursuing. Bullmrkte will allow students to be better prepare for the job market and help them find a meaningful career.

sentiment_very_satisfied Career roadmaps for students to figure out what they will need to learn and how to get into a specific career

sentiment_very_satisfied A network of seasoned professionals that will video chat with students and give them insight on their profession

sentiment_very_satisfied Options for accountability coaches and career counseling services

Discovery Phase

The Process of our design began with understanding the market and our potential end-users. We conducted User Surveys and Competitive Analysis to ensure our solution will satisfy our stakeholders. Below are some of the results from our interviews and surveys.

80% of professionals surveyed did not know what they wanted to do with their careers in high school or college

60% of professionals surveyed have careers that do not align with their college degree

Fitness is the most popular app in the accountability/coaching space, so that leaves room for growth in the career accountability/coaching space

50% of users surveyed would like free services from a coaching/accountability app

vpn_key

Key Takeaways from User Surveys & User Research

vpn_key There is a marketplace for an app that helps students of all ages with career roadmaps, mentorships, and accountability

vpn_key 60% of professionals are in careers that do not align with their college degree

vpn_key The app must provide some type of free service in order to attract users

Competitive Analysis

vpn_key

Key Takeaways from Competitive Analysis


Coach.me has brand recognition and has a free habit tracker that comes with the app. The typical coaching starts off at $14.99/week.

StickK makes you sign a contract that if you do not meet your goals you have to pay money to a non-profit

Beeminder makes you sign a contract that if you do not meet your goals you have to pay money to a non-profit

Competitive Summary: Looking at all the competitors we felt we had to differentiate ourselves. We wanted to provide career roadmaps for the top careers that will actually make people money and earn a liveable income. Bullmrkte will focus on the niche market of careers and mentorship.

vpn_key Focus on career roadmaps and mentorships

vpn_key Offer other services such as goal tracking, career services, and accountability coaching

vpn_key Provide industry insights on the top jobs and skills needed for the current market

vpn_key

Key Takeaways from the Discovery phase

There are so many apps and platforms in the coaching and accountability space. After conducting our user surveys, interviews and competitive analysis we decided we wanted to focus a niche in order to succeed. Our competitive market strategy was to focus on career roadmaps and mentorships. We would add other services as our MVP product moved along including goal tracking, career services, and industry trends analysis.

vpn_key We need to create a brand niche and competitive advantage by focusing on students in high school, college, and adult learners

vpn_key Focus on free career roadmaps and paid mentorships for our MVP product

vpn_key Add in other services later such as goal tracking, career services, and industry trends analysis.

Information Architecture

Once we concluded the Discovery phase we felt comfortable to move on to the Information Architecture phase. Below are the deliverables created.

  • John
  • Age: 19
  • Occupation: Student

  • Bio: John is a student at George Mason University and he is majoring in Accounting. He is a member of the basketball team and is very excited about his future.

  • Motivation: John is a driven individual and wants the best career possible for his future. He was told Accounting has good job prospects right out of college and is a good degree to pursue.

  • Goals: John wants to make sure Accounting is the best major for him and not waste time on a degree that will not make him happy in the future. His goals are to have a successful career in the future that will provide him a livable income. John always had a passion for technology bus is not sure if there is a future for him in the technology world.

  • Frustrations: John is not sure if Accounting will be the most fulfilling career in the future and is only majoring in it because his father has a successful career as an Accountant and the job prospects are good. John wants to look at other options but does not know where to start or who to go to for advice.
  • Stacey
  • Age: 26
  • Occupation: Full-Stack Developer

  • Bio: Stacey was a graduate of Harvard and majored in Computer Science. She currently works for a major government contracting firm as a Full-Stack Developer. She has always had a passion for technology.

  • Motivation: Stacey always wanted to be a teacher, but she knew the pay was not the best. She has a passion to help younger students out with their career.

  • Goals: Stacey wants to help mentor students for the future of the tech industry and if she can make some extra money that would be a bonus. Her goals are to help students figure out their talents early on and save time on finding their true passions in life so that they can have a satisfying career.

  • Frustrations: Stacey has no idea where to start in her search for a platform to help students. She also does not have time to find students to mentor. If only there was a platform that could allow her to coach mentor students for the future of tech.

Conclusion

What worked?

Focusing on the MVP product of career roadmaps and mentorship opportunities first. All the additional requirements can be added in later. goal setting, accountability coaching, career services, and job trends.

Always try to create something functional as soon as possible and get it tested. Refinements can always be made in later iterations.

What didn’t work?

Using to many branding colors with the UI. To much red was overwhelming for users eyes.

What I learned?

For the UI its is always best to try to stick with greys and whites with splashes of branding color for CAB buttons or secondary buttons.
